Most Iconic Music Festivals In The World

Coachella

Coachella

Coachella is an annual music and arts event held in California's Coachella Valley. It usually spans two weekends in April and has a varied lineup of musical performances, from well-known headliners to up-and-coming performers. In addition to music, the event features art installations and sculptures, as well as food and beverage vendors. Coachella is now a global phenomenon that attracts visitors from all over the globe.

Glastonbury Festival

Glastonbury Festival

A well-known annual music and arts celebration, Glastonbury celebration is hosted in Glastonbury, England. It includes a diverse lineup of musical acts from a variety of genres as well as other artistic and cultural activities. It has established itself as a cultural establishment both in the UK and abroad thanks to its innovative social and environmental projects, distinct atmosphere, and sense of community.

Summerfest

Summerfest

Over 800 acts from various genres, including rock, pop, hip-hop, and country, perform at the annual Summerfest music event in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, which lasts 11 days. Over 700,000 people attend it annually, making it the biggest music event in the world. Summerfest offers more than just music; it also has food stands, engaging displays, and other attractions. The event, which takes place on the shores of Lake Michigan, is a Midwest summertime highlight.

Tomorrowland

Tomorrowland

In Boom, Belgium, there is a yearly electronic dance music festival called Tomorrowland, which draws about 400,000 people. Top DJs, numerous stages, and EDM genres like trance, house, and techno are all featured. The festival is well-known for its theatrical stage performances that are both immersive and interesting and feature stunning set designs and visual effects.
